WHY IS TRAINING YOUR PET IMPORTANT? BEHAVIOURAL AND OBEDIENCE TRAINING
TRAINING AT DOGGIIE DOG WORLD PUPPY SCHOOL AT DOGGIIE DOG WORLD

Dogs Are Loyal, Show Unconditional Affection And Have An Inherent Zest For Life.

  • Moreover, they are social animals. In order to live happy, healthy lives they need regular social interaction with not only humans, but also others of their own kind.
  • Despite their social tendencies, they are different from you and me. They can demonstrate many innocent yet vexing tendencies like jumping on people, chewing and barking, and also become withdrawn and depressed. It is critical therefore that they have proper training in order to be accepted socially by friends and family.
  • Training your dog and at the same time learning how to do it yourself, will enhance your bond with your pet, ensure its safety and provide happiness. It is important that your dog understands how you would like it to behave and why it is in its best interest that all commands as dictated by you are followed.

There Are Two Types Of Training –
Obedience

Obedience training is training your puppy to obey certain commands such as sit, stay, come, as well as teaching it to heel and walk. Since pups have a short attention span, the training sessions should be short, 10-15 minutes, two to three times a day to prevent it from becoming bored.

Behavioural

Behavioural training incorporates discipline in your pet. It prevents as well as corrects bad habits such as jumping on people, begging for food, climbing on sofas or chewing furniture. Consistency during the training process is important, ensuring that you do not allow the puppy to do anything you don't want it doing even in the future. For example, if you are not planning on allowing your pet to lay on the couch when it is fully grown, then it is important that you start training it to obey that command from the beginning. Contradictory commands can confuse the pup and cause problems.

All Puppies Need Socialisation Regardless Of Breed, Type, Or Temperament.

Socialisation is the process of making your dog familiar with the environment, people, places, and other animals, as well as learning how to behave in society. While a social dog will blossom into a happy, confident and secure dog who is comfortable in all situations, an under-socialised dog can grow up to be timid and possibly frightened of new things.
